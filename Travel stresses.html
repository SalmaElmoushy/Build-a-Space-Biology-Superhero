What stresses does your body face when being on a trip to space ?

Most people think that astronauts ' job is all fun as they get to explore new things and live an extremely unique experience that not many can go through , which is right !

Nevertheless , astronauts actually face a lot of hardships , whether those hardships are mentally or physically . The change of the normal circumstances that our body is designed to live in , horribly changes the functions of our body's organs .

A human body functions best while being on Earth , because of air pressure , gravity , good nutrition , and being exposed to just the right amount and type of radiation that passes through Earth's atmosphere .

  • Space anemia: As we know, blood represents 7% of a human’s body weight. It is responsible for transporting nutrition, gases, and wastes. Blood consists of three components, which are: red blood cells; white blood cells; and plasma. Focusing on the RBCs, our body loses 2 million RBCs per second while in normal circumstances. The bone marrow, which is responsible for producing new ones, can easily compensate for this amount. However, in space, the body is being put into harder circumstances where gravity doesn’t help in blood circulation and is offered fewer fluids.
